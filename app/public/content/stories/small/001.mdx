I <u>see</u> a <u>fox</u>. The <u>fox</u> is <u>red</u>. The <u>red</u> <u>fox</u> can <u>run</u>. It can <u>run</u> and <u>hop</u>. The <u>fox</u> can <u>hop</u> <u>on</u> a <u>log</u>. 

The <u>sun</u> is <u>hot</u>. The <u>fox</u> <u>sits</u> by the <u>oak</u> tree. It is a <u>big</u> <u>oak</u> tree. The <u>fox</u> can <u>nap</u> <u>in</u> the shade. It likes to <u>nap</u> <u>when</u> the <u>sun</u> is <u>hot</u>.

A <u>bug</u> is <u>on</u> the <u>log</u>. The <u>bug</u> is tiny. The <u>fox</u> can <u>see</u> the <u>bug</u>. The <u>bug</u> can <u>hop</u> <u>too</u>. It hops away from the <u>fox</u>. The <u>fox</u> <u>lets</u> the <u>bug</u> <u>go</u>.

<u>Now</u> the <u>fox</u> <u>gets</u> <u>up</u>. It is time to <u>run</u> home. The <u>fox</u> runs to its <u>den</u>. The <u>den</u> is <u>in</u> the hill. The <u>fox</u> is happy to be home.
